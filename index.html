St. Dominic (1170–1221) was a Spanish priest and the founder of the Dominican Order (Order of Preachers). He was known for his strong faith, deep learning, and powerful preaching. Dominic believed in spreading the truth of the Gospel through education, prayer, and example.

He lived at a time when the Church faced many challenges, including heresies (false teachings). Instead of using force, Dominic chose to preach with love and reason. He and his followers lived simply, trusting in God's providence.

St. Aloysius Gonzaga (1568–1591) was an Italian Jesuit and is the patron saint of young students and Christian youth. Born into a noble family, he gave up a life of wealth and privilege to dedicate himself fully to God. At a young age, he joined the Society of Jesus (Jesuits), despite opposition from his family.

Aloysius was known for his deep prayer life, humility, and care for the poor and sick. During a plague outbreak in Rome, he worked tirelessly to care for victims, even though it risked his own health. He eventually caught the disease himself and died at the age of 23.

Teachers are the heart of every school. They do more than just teach lessons—they inspire, guide, and shape us into who we are becoming. Through their patience, dedication, and hard work, they help us understand not only academic subjects, but also life values such as respect, discipline, and perseverance. Teachers spend countless hours preparing lessons, checking our work, and finding ways to help us improve. They believe in our potential even when we doubt ourselves, and they push us to do our best. Whether they’re strict or fun, quiet or outgoing, each teacher leaves a lasting impact on our lives. They don’t just prepare us for exams—they prepare us for the future.

The waiting shed is where students gather to wait for their parents or their rides home. It’s more than just a place to wait, it’s a spot full of energy and friendship. During these moments, laughter and chatter fill the air as students share stories, jokes, and experiences from their day. It’s a time when friendships grow stronger and bonds are made, making the waiting feel shorter and more enjoyable. The waiting shed is truly a small community where we connect and unwind before heading home.

Elementary (or grade) school is where our learning journey begins. It’s a place full of discovery, fun, and new friendships. Here, we build the foundation of important skills like reading, writing, and basic math, while also learning how to work with others and develop good habits. It’s a time of curiosity and growth, where teachers guide us with patience and kindness, helping us become confident learners ready for the challenges ahead.

Our homeroom class feels like a second family where we share laughter, support, and sometimes even extra math time, but it’s always fun learning together. With our adviser’s guidance, we grow not just as students, but as friends. It’s a place where we belong, no matter how busy school gets.

The medical mission is a meaningful event where we come together to help our community by providing free health check-ups and basic medical care. It’s a chance for us to give back, learn about health awareness, and see the importance of compassion and service. Through this mission, we not only support those in need but also grow as responsible and caring individuals.

School events play an important role in creating a lively and engaging atmosphere within the campus. They bring students, teachers, and even parents together to celebrate achievements, showcase talents, and build stronger connections within the school community. From academic competitions and sports fests to cultural performances and outreach programs, these events offer opportunities for students to grow outside the classroom. They promote teamwork, creativity, leadership, and school spirit, making each moment memorable and meaningful. Whether it’s a fun celebration or a formal recognition ceremony, school events are a vital part of every student’s journey.

School facilities play a big role in creating a comfortable and effective learning environment for students. From classrooms and libraries to computer labs, science rooms, and sports areas, each facility supports our growth in different ways. Clean and well-maintained classrooms help us focus on our lessons, while libraries give us access to knowledge beyond our textbooks. Computer and science labs allow us to explore and apply what we learn through hands-on experiences. Even simple areas like the canteen, restrooms, and open spaces contribute to our well-being and daily routines. Good school facilities show that the school values our education, health, and safety—and they make learning more enjoyable and productive.

Sir Jess and Sir Carlo are our computer subject teachers. They’re known for being strict when it comes to deadlines and requirements, which pushes us to be more disciplined and responsible. However, despite their strictness, they genuinely care about our progress. When they see that we’re making an effort but struggling, they’re willing to extend deadlines because they don’t want any of us to fail. Their goal is to help us learn, improve, and succeed, not just pass the subject.

As Lousians, we carry more than just a name, we carry a sense of pride, discipline, and unity. Being a Louisian means striving for excellence in everything we do, whether it’s in academics, sports, leadership, or service. Our school doesn’t just teach us lessons from books; it teaches us values that shape who we are—respect, integrity, faith, and compassion. We are guided by passionate teachers, supported by strong friendships, and surrounded by a community that encourages us to grow. Every event, every challenge, and every achievement reminds us that we are part of something bigger. We’re not just students, we are Lousians, and we represent our school with heart, honor, and hope for the future.
